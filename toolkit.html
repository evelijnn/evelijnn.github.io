Visual research

Visual research is the use of things like images, video and objects for inspiration or reference.

Media to think of for visual research:
- Movies / film / videos / gif
- Photography
- Paintings / drawings / illustrations
- Performance / theater / dance etc.
- Daily life
- Illustrative books (probably already covered in the other points)
- Games
How to use it?
> visit museums, galleries, exhibitions, theater, cinema, festivals

> When you gather the visuals you have to start to analyze them. You should actively think about what you want to use it for and how you can learn that. Also see the Personal examples for more specific examples.

> So for example I would use it to see meaning, color, composition, light and movement.

> It is always useful to compare visuals from different artist, you can try to figure out different working methods and how this leads to the different outcomes.
When to use it?
- When looking for inspiration

- When looking for references

Literary research

Literary research is the use of media for gaining knowledge about subjects and diving deeper in the subject/theme

Media to think of for literary research:
- Articles
- Newspaper
- Books
- Letters
- Social media
- Magazines
- Analysis video's
- Other's interviews
- Talks (like TED)

average
How to use it?
> You need to know what you want to research.

> You start of by reading or listening. Read actively and take notes and think of questions when reading or listening. With these questions you dive deeper in the subject.
> You also have to validate your sources.
For validation it is important to find multiple opinions(I don't think opinion covers exactly what I mean but I'm not sure what other word to use) and use different kind of sources to check who says what and what opinion you want to take.
> It is also important to look at the referenced research and sites that your source has used, this way you can see where they get their information from.
>Then you have to reflect and see where you stand.

Personal examples
How to use it?
> Ask questions to gain people's opinion, attitude, knowledge and feelings.

> It is important to ask the right questions; you have to know what you want to know from your subject in relation to your project and with what questions you can obtain that information.

> Different interview types to think of
- face to face
- form / survey
- phone / video call

> Ask open questions starting with who, what, where, when and why.
- When looking for inspiration

- When looking for references

- When you want to know more about current developments


When to use it?
When to use it?
- When looking for inspiration

- When needing references

- When you want to know people's opinion / experience

Research by doing is all the research you do by making stuff / acting things out.

Actions to think of when making research by doing:
- Drawing / painting / crafting
- Acting / dancing / singing / talking / making sounds

> Drawing what is in your head

> Experimenting with materials

> Tryouts of your project to see multiple outcomes

> acting out movement for animation

> It is very important to just start creating, you shouldn't keep your thoughts and images in your head, you need to start drawing and creating to start finding what you're looking for. The things you create won't really be how you imagine it in your mind but it will help you to see more options of what is possible and it will inspire you to develop your project.
How to use it?
When to use it?

- When asking for feedback

- When you need a different insight
